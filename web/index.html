Open-source
film scan editor.

OpenEnlarge is free, open-source software that brings the darkroom to your desktop. Instead of naively flipping colors, it simulates the actual chemistry of film and paper — grounded in the Beer-Lambert density model the math is drawn from — to invert your scans authentically and edit them, all in one fast, fluid workflow.

Features

A real darkroom, on your desktop.

🎞️

Cineon density inversion

A physically-based Beer-Lambert engine (Kodak Cineon / negadoctor) inverts in the density domain, anchored on the measured film base — not a flipped curve.

🔄

Negative & positive, auto

Every frame is classified on develop — negatives are inverted, positives pass through untouched. One click overrides either way. Pin D_max precisely by clicking a clear-leader patch in the live viewport.

📥

RAW, TIFF, JPEG & PNG

Fuji RAF, Panasonic RW2, Nikon NEF, Sony ARW, Canon CR3, Hasselblad 3FR and DNG, plus 16-bit TIFF, JPEG and PNG — decoded to linear RGB.

📷

Tethered shooting

Watch a folder and auto-import + develop new scans as they land — finished positives appear as you shoot.

🧪

Automatic film-base detection

Finds the orange-mask rebate and samples it as one coherent clear-film color, locked per roll. Measured inside your crop, so camera scans don't wash out — recalibrate or pick a neutral anytime.

🎚️

Full develop controls

Tonal curves, color grading, color wheels, exposure, black point and gamma — live. Copy and paste tone & color between frames.

🎨

Tone Matching

Match the toning of a frame to any reference image, with an adjustable strength — borrow the mood of a look you love.

🪄

AI Enhance

One-click enhancement powered by OpenAI (gpt-image-2). Bring your own API key in Settings and enhance straight from the toolbar.

🔍

Upscale to 4K / 8K

On-device upscaling with a tiled ONNX engine — models download on demand, no cloud round-trip. Standalone, or as a finishing pass on a developed frame.

🧹

AI Dust & Hair Removal

Automatic defect detection plus MI-GAN inpainting — or paint a mask with the AI-fill eraser and apply a single, undoable AI erase.

✂️

Crop & batch export

Straighten, crop and rotate with a live histogram, then batch export to 16-bit TIFF, PNG or JPEG — with one shared crop applied across the whole roll.

☀️

HDR preview & export

Toggle any frame into true HDR — highlights glow beyond white on HDR-capable displays — and export it as a gain-map HDR JPEG that matches the preview. Experimental.

⌨️

Headless CLI

film-cli runs the same density engine for scripting and batch inversion.

How it works

Density first, aesthetics second.

01

Decode

Your RAF/DNG/TIFF scan is decoded to linear RGB — the light the scanner actually measured through the film.

02

Invert in density

Restore each channel's density relative to the measured film base, anchored to the roll's density range, then print back to a positive (Kodak Cineon). Density is linear in dye; transmittance isn't — so this is where naive flips go wrong.

03

Develop

Apply creative finishing — curves, color, exposure — on a faithful base. Export, or batch the whole roll.
